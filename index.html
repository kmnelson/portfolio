About Me

I am a physics PhD with a strong interest in developing and applying new AI methods to advance science. A particular highlight of this research is a new method for fine-tuning transformers to use p-values as loss functions. I also introduced a new method for multi-task learning which improves performance by running tasks in series rather than parallel, inspired by the weight-sharing in CNNs.

I have also worked at CERN as a software engineer and technical lead on a project to automate data quality monitoring using containerized pipelines deployed at scale for a pipeline with 2 GB/s data throughput. The new web dashboard summarizes the results of hundreds of statistical tests on the data to alert scientists more quickly about possible bugs or changes in detector operating conditions, allowing for thousands of scientists to publish more quickly.
